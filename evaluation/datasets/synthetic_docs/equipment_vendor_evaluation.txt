Equipment Vendor Evaluation Report — Regional Healthcare Consortium

Report Date: March 15, 2025
Prepared by: Marcus Webb, VP of Operations, Regional Healthcare Consortium
Vendors Evaluated: TechVita Medical Systems, NovaCare Equipment

TechVita Medical Systems:
Current Contracts:
- Riverside General Hospital: Hematology analyzer (HemaFlow 9000), chemistry analyzer (ChemVita 5500), and point-of-care testing devices. Annual service contract value: $285,000.
- St. Marcus Medical Center: Blood gas analyzers and urinalysis systems. Contract value: $142,000.

Performance Issues (Q1 2025):
The HemaFlow 9000 firmware failure at Riverside (March 7-10) resulted in delayed lab results for multiple patients. Field service response time was 18 hours, exceeding the contractual SLA of 4 hours. TechVita attributed the delay to technician availability in the northeast region.

NovaCare Equipment:
Current Contracts:
- St. Marcus Medical Center: NeuroScan 3T MRI system (installed February 2025). Annual service contract: $420,000. System experienced calibration issues during March 8-9, causing scheduling delays for neurology patients including a referred epilepsy case from Riverside General.
- Riverside General Hospital: Currently evaluating NovaCare as backup pathology equipment vendor per Dr. Anita Desai's recommendation following the TechVita incident.

Recommendation:
The consortium recommends dual-vendor strategies for critical diagnostic equipment. Both vendors have been notified that SLA penalties will be enforced for Q2 2025 violations. A formal RFP for backup hematology and imaging equipment will be issued by April 1, 2025.
