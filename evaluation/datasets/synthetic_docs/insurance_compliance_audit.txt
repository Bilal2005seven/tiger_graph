Insurance Provider Compliance Audit — Regional Summary

Audit Period: Q1 2025 (January — March)
Auditor: Regional Healthcare Compliance Office
Date Published: March 20, 2025

Provider: BlueCrest Health Insurance
Claims Processed (Riverside General + St. Marcus): 4,217
Claims Denied: 312 (7.4% denial rate, up from 5.1% in Q4 2024)

Notable Denial Patterns:
- Prior authorization failures: 48% of denials. BlueCrest tightened pre-authorization requirements for chemotherapy and advanced imaging effective January 2025 without adequate provider notification.
- Case Example: Maria Santos (Policy #BC-502716) — Cyclophosphamide claim denied despite completed PET-CT staging. Denial cited missing documentation that was in fact submitted but not processed by BlueCrest within their own 5-day review window. Peer-to-peer review between Dr. Priya Sharma and BlueCrest Medical Director Dr. Howard Chen is pending.

Provider: Meridian Life Coverage
Claims Processed: 3,891
Claims Denied: 198 (5.1% denial rate, stable)

Notable Cases:
- Clara Nguyen (Policy #MLC-773012): ICU stay approved retroactively after initial denial. Meridian initially classified the adverse drug reaction as a "preventable event" and attempted to shift liability to Riverside General. Hospital Risk Management successfully appealed citing systemic HIE data failure.
- David Kowalski (Policy #MLC-881204): Emergency department and ICU claims approved without issue. Ongoing Warfarin management claims under review for medical necessity documentation.

Provider: National Care Alliance
Claims Processed: 1,204
Claims Denied: 61 (5.1%)
- Robert Fitzgerald (Policy #NCA-558190): Inter-facility transfer from Riverside to St. Marcus approved. Ongoing EEG monitoring and MRI claims pending review.

Recommendation: Escalate BlueCrest denial rate trend to the Regional Health Authority for formal inquiry.
